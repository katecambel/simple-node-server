How Tumblr Works

Tumblr is another microblogging platform that let's users share posts, images, videos, music. The website was invented by Internet entrepreneur David Karp and lead designer Marco Arment in 2007. What I love about tumblr is that users can reblog other people's posts as well, but with an acclaimed authorization, hence it is as though tumblr bloggers are just sharing their thoughts. Tumblr bloggers then get to find other bloggers that have the same thoughts, opinions, and inspirations as others. Other elements of Tumblr are being able to give notes to one another's posts, starting a conversation. One could also heart or reblog a post.

This is what differs and why I prefer Tumblr to Facebook and Twitter, because in Tumblr, one could be totally anonymous. It is also a very visual and creative website. And as Tumblr users would commonly say, Tumblr has its own world. Users have coined the terms "tumblring" or "tumblrlogging" to refer to simply being active on the site. Hence most people don’t know that Tumblr has been quietly growing enormously, having 33 million users with over 12 billion posts. About 25,000 new users sign up and about 71.6 million new entries are posted each day. It is the 10th largest social network with over 6.8 million weekly visits.

But how does Tumblr make money? Through its advertisements, and recently Yahoo has bought Tumblr. But the way advertisement works in Tumblr is that David Karp wanted the ads that aim to win awards by selling stories not to have banner apps. He wants the advertisements to go with the flow of Tumblr's system, which is a continuous feed (or Dashboard) of creative posts, images and of other media. But unlike other microblogs, there is minimal ads found on site.
