Early World Wide Web

It was during the 1990s when it all began.

But the Web and the Internet are two different things - they catch different things; and the Web is one global sea.

In March 1989, Tim Berners-Lee wrote a paper entitled Information Management: A Proposal that noted links such as references, which are far more useful than a fixed hierarchal system. He then proposed a web - which is a hypertext + the Internet - to connect information. The Hypertext already existed but documents are in a non-linear way.

In 1991, he eventually invented the World Wide Web, which is accessible today to anyone with a browser and Internet. The most popular browsers today are Firefox, Google Chrome and Safar.

But in 1992, Erwise was the first internet browser and the first to have a graphical interface.

In 1993, Mosaic was the second browser to come around, the most popular then that influenced many browsers to follow suit.

In 1994, Netscape Navigator was invented and took up 90% of web usage in the year after.

In the early 90s, the AOL or the first dial-up internet was invented, connecting it to the internet via a telephone line-modem.

Fun fact, pizza was the first thing purchased on the internet.

Another fun fact, Tim Berners-Lee's computer can be found in the London Science Museum.

And another fun fact, on average a webpage is 19 clicks away from every other webpage.

Lastly, 80% of the information on the Web are not yet indexed.
