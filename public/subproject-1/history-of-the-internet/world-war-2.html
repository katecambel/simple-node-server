During the WWII

50 years ago, computers were used in the cold war but it took so much space.

Before 1957, there was batch processing which was proven ineffective. Computers got bigger throughout the years that stored special cooled rums. Programming had a lot of manual work but also consisted a lot of bugs.

In 1957, a remote connection had to be installed so developers could work directly on the computer. The idea of time-sharing then came up which is the first concept in computer technology by share processing of one computer with multiple users.

On October 4, 1957, during the height of the cold war, the first satellite was sent into the orbit by the Soviet Union named Sputnik I - the actual first satellite sent into space.

In February 1958, the United States of America government led by Dwighty Eisenhower founded DARPA - Defense Advanced Research Project Agency. This is the branch that focused specifically on developing technologies for the protection of the US. Hence, DARPA’s (or ARPA or ARPANET) focus was solely on computer. The computer network has to be in place in case a nuclear attack were to take place. Hence it looks at nuclear radiation through its electro-magnetic waves. The ICBMs or Intercontinental Ballistic Missiles then had a range that could go from Germany to Britain.

Then, people were the only means to transfer new knowledge.

ARPANET - [Defense] Advanced Research Project Agency

There were 3 concepts made then:

  • RAND (Rand Corporation) from military in the US
  • NPL (National Physical Laboratory) in England
  • CYCLADES (Institut de Recherche d' Informatique et d' Automatique) in France

Fun fact, In the Philippines, PLDT or Philippine Long Distance Telephone Company has the biggest monopoly that explain why the country has one of the slowest internet connections in the world.
